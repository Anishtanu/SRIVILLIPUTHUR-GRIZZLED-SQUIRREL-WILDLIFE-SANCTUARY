About Srivilliputhur Grizzled Squirrel Wildlife Sanctuary

The Srivilliputhur Grizzled Squirrel Wildlife Sanctuary is a beautiful protected forest area in Tamil Nadu, near the town of Srivilliputhur. It was started in 1988 to protect the Grizzled Giant Squirrel, a rare and endangered animal found only in a few parts of South India and Sri Lanka.

The sanctuary covers about 480 square kilometers and has thick forests, hills, rivers, and waterfalls. It is home to many wild animals like elephants, leopards, monkeys, deer, and different kinds of birds and butterflies.

This sanctuary is very important because it helps to protect wildlife and keep nature safe. It also connects with nearby forests like Periyar Tiger Reserve and Megamalai Wildlife Sanctuary, allowing animals to move freely in their natural habitat.

People visit this place to enjoy the beauty of nature, go trekking, watch birds, and learn more about forest life and animal conservation.

The vegetation of the sanctuary varies with altitude and climate, ranging from evergreen forests at higher elevations to dry deciduous forests in the lower regions. Major plant types found here include:

  • Tropical Evergreen Trees: Found in moist areas ‚Äî Hopea parviflora, Mesua ferrea, Terminalia paniculata, and Pterocarpus marsupium.

  • Bamboo & Grasslands: Dense patches of Bambusa arundinacea and various grass species form habitats for grazing herbivores.
